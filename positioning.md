## Name candidates

Picking the brand. Criteria: short, easy to spell over a phone, memorable, .in available, no negative connotations in Hindi/regional languages.

### Top 5 to consider

| Name | Why it works | Why it might not |
|---|---|---|
| **PropCheck** | Direct, descriptive, says exactly what it does. | Generic; many similar names exist globally. |
| **TrustProp** | Leads with trust — the core promise. Verb-y. | Slightly clinical. |
| **RealRadar** | Memorable, image of scanning, alliterative. | "Radar" feels military / techy; less warm. |
| **TrueBricks** | Plays off "Magicbricks" — anti-establishment hook. Feels Indian. | Risk of trademark scuffle with Magicbricks. |
| **ScanProp** | Action-verb + property. Fits the Chrome-extension scan moment. | Less brandable than Trust*. |

### Other shortlisted

- ListingSafe — descriptive but long
- PropDoctor — fun but consumer-facing only
- VerifiedFlats — descriptive, narrow (only flats)
- SaafProperty — Hindi "saaf" (clean) + property; bilingual hook
- BrickGuard — protective tone; slightly aggressive

### Recommendation
Start with **PropCheck** if `propcheck.in` is available. If not, **TrustProp**. Both are direct, both grow with you, both work for B2C and B2B audiences.

**Action:** check domain availability for top 5 today. Pick within 24 hours. Don't overthink — Stripe was once "/dev/payments", Twitter was twttr, names are the easiest thing to change later.

## What we are / what we are not

**We are:**
- Neutral. Independent. Buyer-side.
- A check before a transaction.
- Data + signals + clear next steps.
- B2C free, B2B API revenue.

**We are not:**
- A property portal. We don't list flats.
- A broker. We don't take commissions.
- A lawyer. We don't certify titles (Landeed handles that — we partner).
- A guarantee. We surface signals; the buyer still does final due-diligence.

This separation matters legally and brand-wise. Don't blur it.

## Brand voice

### Voice principles

1. **Calm, not alarmist.** We're warning users, not scaring them. "Risky" not "DANGER!!!"
2. **Direct, not clever.** A buyer in panic mode wants clarity, not wordplay.
3. **Numbers and reasons.** Every claim ("Price 22% below average") has a source.
4. **Plain English / Hinglish.** No "actionable insights", no "cutting-edge AI". Just say what we did.
5. **Buyer-first.** When in doubt, ask: would a 35-year-old engineer in Bangalore buying her first flat understand and trust this line?

### Words we use
verify, check, score, signal, flag, listing, locality, builder, title, deed, RERA, fair price, scam, deposit, token

### Words we avoid
- "Cutting-edge", "AI-powered" *(everyone says it; means nothing)*
- "Revolutionizing real estate" *(grandiose; we're a checker)*
- "Trusted by millions" until we are
- "Disrupting" anything
- Hindi-English mash without intent ("aapka property check karein") — only use Hinglish where it adds warmth, not as decoration

### Tone examples

**Bad** *(too techy):*
> Our proprietary AI-driven trust verification engine leverages multi-source data fusion to deliver actionable property intelligence.

**Bad** *(too alarmist):*
> 🚨 SCAM ALERT! This listing is 100% FAKE. Don't lose your money!

**Good:**
> This listing has 4 red flags. The biggest one: photos appear on 7 other listings, which usually means they're stolen. Visit in person before paying any token.

## Visual identity (rough direction for designer brief)

- **Primary colour**: a calm trust-blue (think Stripe, Wise, Cleartrip).
- **Accent**: green for "Safe" scores, amber for "Caution", red for "Risky". Never use red as decoration — only for warnings.
- **Typography**: a clean sans-serif (Inter, Manrope, or DM Sans). No "luxury real estate" serifs.
- **Iconography**: minimal line icons. Big checkmarks. No stock photos of happy families in front of flats.
- **Hero image**: ideally a real screenshot of the product, not lifestyle photography.

The whole brand should feel like a security tool (Truecaller, 1Password, Cred's safety messaging) — not like a real estate brochure.

## Audience segments

### Primary — Free users
- 28–45, urban, first-time or second-time property buyer/renter.
- Earning ₹8L–60L/year. Salaried or self-employed.
- Already searches on Magicbricks, 99acres, Housing, NoBroker.
- Has been burned (or knows someone who has).
- WhatsApp-native. Will forward useful tools to family group.

### Primary — Paying B2B
- Home loan teams at HDFC, SBI, ICICI, Axis, Bajaj Finserv.
- Insurance underwriters for home/title insurance.
- Property law firms doing pre-purchase due-diligence.
- Eventually: RERA authorities themselves.

### Secondary — Pro users
- NRIs buying property in India remotely (high pain, high willingness to pay).
- Investors with 3+ properties tracking listings.
- Real estate journalists / researchers.
